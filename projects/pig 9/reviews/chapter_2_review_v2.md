## Chapter 2 Analysis: Brightville Bound

**1. Internal Consistency**

*   **Issue 1:** In Scene 1, Wilbur "skirted the chicken coop, the scent of feathers and droppings making him wrinkle his nose." Later in Scene 3, while on the truck, he's "been crammed in with squawking chickens." This contradicts the earlier aversion to chickens/their scent. While Wilbur wouldn't be thrilled at this situation, it runs counter to the negative reaction established earlier.
*   **Issue 2:** Scene 3 - Wilbur mentions Old Man Hemlock's grumbling. This character hasn't been introduced and their presence is not immediately understandable to the audience.

**2. Clarity**

*   **Issue 1:** Scene 2 - The description "the darkness within the truck cab was broken only by slivers of light piercing through gaps in the wooden slats" is confusing since Wilbur is in the back, amongst crates, and not in the cab of the truck. This sentence placement and word choice are misleading.
*   **Issue 2:** In Scene 4, it's mentioned he "borrowed" a denim jacket. The implications and logistics of this borrowing (from the barn? from a person?) are unclear and interrupt the flow of his feelings of desperation.

**3. Plot Holes**

*   **Issue 1:** In Scene 1, it's unrealistic that a delivery truck would have "a slightly ajar panel at the back, designed for ventilation" large enough for a pig to squeeze through. This is especially odd considering it is filled with produce. Ventilation holes would be small to prevent contamination and theft.
*   **Issue 2:** Scene 3: It seems unlikely no one would notice a pig getting out of the back of a delivery truck, especially in a bustling market. The description of the unloading process doesn't make it plausible that Wilbur could slip away unnoticed.

**4. Redundancy**

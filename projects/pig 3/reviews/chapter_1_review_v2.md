### 5. Flow and Transitions
- Scene 2 feels slightly disjointed from Scene 1. While Scene 1 sets up Johnny's desire for nature and isolation, Scene 2 abruptly throws him into a crowded street without a clear indication of how much time has passed or what his intention was after leaving the apartment. This makes the scene feel a bit random and disconnected from Johnny's emotional state established in Scene 1.
- Scene 3 has an abrupt transition. It feels like it starts mid-action, without a proper lead-in from the end of Scene 2. The reader is suddenly in an alley without knowing how Johnny got there or why he chose that location.

### 6. Actionable Fixes
1. **(Flow/Transitions - Scene 2)**: Add a sentence or two at the end of Scene 1 indicating Johnny's destination or intention for his walk. For example: "He decided to walk to Oakhaven Central Park, hoping to find a patch of green amidst the grey."
2. **(Flow/Transitions - Scene 3)**: Add a sentence or two connecting Scene 2 to Scene 3. For example: "Frustrated by the crowds on Elm Street, Johnny decided to cut through a narrow alley he knew led to the back of 'Sweet Surrender' bakery."
3. **(Grammar - Scene 4)**: Fix the spelling error in the header for Scene 4: "Arnie, a pig with a penchant f... for rhythmn" should be "Arnie, a pig with a penchant for rhythm."
4. **(Formatting - Scene 5)**: Remove the duplicated Scene 5 header.

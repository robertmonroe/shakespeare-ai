# Book Summary
Percy, a genetically modified pig with an uncanny vocal resemblance to Elvis Presley, dreams of escaping his bacon-destined life on an Iowa farm. He discovers his talent by chance and plots a daring escape, hoping to trade mud baths for rhinestones. He makes it to Memphis.

In Memphis, he meets Daisy, a runaway teen with a talent for sewing and a love of Elvis, who becomes his unlikely ally. Together, they navigate the world of Elvis impersonators, where Percy, rebranded as "The Swine-King," finds unexpected fame. However, the farm's owner is in pursuit, forcing Percy to choose between the spotlight and his freedom.

## Chapter 2: Rhinestones and Runaways
### Summary
Percy successfully escapes the farm and, after a series of improbable events, hitchhikes to Memphis. Lost and confused in the city, he encounters Daisy, a runaway with a knack for sewing Elvis jumpsuits. Daisy recognizes Percy's talent and takes him under her wing, offering him a place to stay and help with crafting his image. They name him "The Swine-King."

## Chapter 3: The Swine-King's Debut
### Summary
Percy makes his debut as "The Swine-King" at a small local club. His unique act becomes an instant hit, drawing crowds and generating buzz. However, back in Iowa, the farmer discovers Percy's escape and sets out to retrieve his prize-winning pig. The farmer arrives in Memphis just as Percy's career is taking off, creating a tense showdown between Percy's newfound fame and his desire for freedom. Percy, with Daisy's help, uses his talent and the support of his fans to outsmart the farmer, securing his freedom and solidifying his place as The Swine-King.

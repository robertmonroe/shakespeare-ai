**5. Flow and Transitions**

*   The shift between Scene 1 and Scene 2 is abrupt. The transition is "Johnny walks home from school..." but Scene 1 ends with Johnny deciding to head out and a feeling that the afternoon won't be like others. It's unclear if he went to school or if he was already out of school.
*   The transition between Scene 2 and Scene 3 is also abrupt. The last sentence of Scene 2 is "He paused, squinting, unsure of what he was seeing." and Scene 3 begins with "The alley behind 'Sweet Surrender' bakery reeked of burnt sugar and stale dough...". This suggests the bakery and what Johnny was seeing are not the same place/thing, which may be confusing to the reader.

**6. Actionable Fixes**

1.  In Scene 1, clarify whether Johnny is heading to school or elsewhere when he leaves the apartment. Add a line or two to establish that he is walking home from school, or otherwise imply it, to connect it to Scene 2.
2.  In Scene 2, ensure that the "flash of pink" is related to, and ideally leads directly to, the alleyway and the discovery of Arnie in Scene 3. If the "flash of pink" has nothing to do with the bakery, remove it, or alter the transition.

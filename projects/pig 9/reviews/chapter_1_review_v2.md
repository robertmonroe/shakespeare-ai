## Chapter 1 Analysis: "Checkmate, Wilbur!"

### 1. Internal Consistency

*   **Issue:** In Scene 2, Wilbur recreates rows like Farmer McGregor using mud, describing the soil as a "canvas demanding order." This is inconsistent with previous descriptions of Wilbur avoiding the muck in Scene 1. It is difficult to imagine Wilbur dipping his snout into the viscous patch of mud.
    *   **Actionable Fix:** Modify Scene 2's description of the mud to be less repellant or modify Wilbur's behavior of recreation into something more in line with his avoidance of the mud.

*   **Issue:** In Scene 3, Wilbur, noticing how the shadows stretch, exclaims "Checkmate," even though he doesn't know the true meaning of the word. This moment feels very pointed and leading, which takes away from the reveal that he had never seen a real chess board.
    *   **Actionable Fix:** Change Wilbur's line to something related to shapes and patterns but avoid using chess terminology.
